Small cents


Small cents were first minted in 1856 and were first put into circulation a year later in 1857. They are still minted to this day. There have been 6 designs that lasted more than a year. They are the following.



Flying Eagle cent

TThe flying eagle cent was minted from 1856 to 1858. But they were only put into circulation in 1857. On the obverse, it had an eagle that was flying and on the reverse a reef with the words one cent.


Indian Head cent

The Indian Head cent was minted from 1859 to 1909. It has a native American on the obverse and a reef with the words one cent on the reverse.

Lincoln Cents


Wheat Cents

Wheat cent was minted from 1909-1958. It has a portrait of Lincoln on the obverse and on the reverse it has 2 leaves of wheat and in between the 2 it says one cent and United States of America.


Memorial Cents

Memorial cent was minted from 1959 to 2008. It has a portrait of Lincoln on the obverse and on the reverse, it has the lincoln memorial.


2009 "commemoratives"

The 2009 "commemoratives" were minted to commemorate the 200 year anniversary of lincoln's birth. They had 4 different designs. 1. Birth and early Childhood witch was a log cabin on the reverse. 2. Formative Years which had Lincoln sitting on a log reading. 3. Professional Life which had Lincoln standing in front of the halls of congress. 4. Presidency which has the white house on the reverse.


Shield cents

Shield cents were minted from 2010 and are still minted to this day. They have a shield on the reverse and a ribbon that says ONE CENT over the shield.
